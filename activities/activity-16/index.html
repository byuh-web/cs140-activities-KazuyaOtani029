History of Japanese Cuisine

Japanese cuisine developed over time through geography, religion, and cultural exchange. Traditional diets were based on rice, fish, and vegetables. Buddhism reduced meat consumption, while foreign influence introduced new ingredients and techniques.

Core Concepts of Japanese Cuisine

Japanese cuisine focuses on simplicity, seasonal ingredients, and natural flavor. Umami is an important taste that defines many dishes.

Learn about Umami

Main Ingredients

Common ingredients include rice, seafood, vegetables, tofu, and seaweed. Soy sauce, miso, and dashi are essential seasonings in Japanese cooking.

Featured Dishes

Ramen

Ramen is a noodle soup dish with rich broth and toppings such as pork, egg, and green onions.

Sushi

Sushi is vinegared rice combined with fish, seafood, or vegetables, often served raw or lightly prepared.

Onigiri

Onigiri are rice balls often filled with salmon or pickled plum and wrapped in seaweed.
