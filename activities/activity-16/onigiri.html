Onigiri (Rice balls)

Onigiri is a traditional Japanese food made by shaping cooked rice into a compact form, often filled with ingredients like salmon or pickled plum and wrapped in seaweed. It is simple, portable, and widely enjoyed in Japan. Wikipedia

History of Onigiri

Onigiri has been eaten in Japan for over a thousand years. It became popular because it is easy to carry and eat, especially for travelers and workers.

How to Make Onigiri

To make onigiri, first cook short-grain Japanese rice and let it cool slightly until it is warm but not too hot to handle. Next, wet your hands with water and lightly sprinkle them with salt to prevent the rice from sticking and to add flavor. Take a portion of rice, place your desired filling such as salmon or pickled plum in the center, and gently shape it into a triangle or oval form. Finally, wrap the onigiri with a sheet of nori (seaweed) for added texture and taste.

For best results, use freshly cooked rice and avoid pressing too hard when shaping, as this can make the onigiri too dense. Keeping your hands moist will help create a smooth surface and prevent sticking.
